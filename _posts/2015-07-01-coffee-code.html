Fueled with a caramel macchiato and Motown playlist, I'm camped out at the local Barnes and Noble continuing to inch through this coding odyssey. I just completed the Firehose Project 2-week prework challenges to get a feel for the program. I've always thought a huge plus of this program in particular was the team project component, so I'm excited to get started. I can't help but continue to window-shop programs because there are so many options, but I keep coming back to this one so I think I'll enroll when I come back from Hong Kong later this month.

I'd also love to take a design class eventually. I keep getting distracted by how amateurish my projects look. Part of it is probably me being obsessive but I also think there's credence to the power of perception. At my previous position, the presentation of my work was almost as important as the solutions I was presenting clients in terms of how they would value my work.

For now, I'm continuing to work through Treehouse and Free Code Camp. I'm really anxious to move through the latter to see what nonprofit projects I might be able to work on. WIRED featured Free Code Camp in this article I came across a few weeks ago, which is when I decided to try it out. Pretty good stuff so far. It's really incredible how alternative coding education has emerged. I wonder if this is something I would have taken advantage of in college, but it might have taken some overwhelming life experiences to jerk me in this new direction I've taken. Carpe diem. :)
