This past weekend, I attended the Madison+ Ruby conference as a student volunteer. It was great to meet experienced Rubyists and hear their views on technology, the community or even guilty pleasure TV shows. :) An unexpected plus--I met 2 fellow Firehosers (@DavidSavatski and @ColinRubbert) who had plenty of advice since they've just wrapped up the program.

[caption id="" align="aligncenter" width="340"] Enjoying some curds and burgers at Buck and Badger[/caption]

My biggest takeaway is that I need to start going to meetups. I met a lot of other bootcamp students (mostly those who came out of the Chicago DevBootcamp), got industry perspective from people in the field, and received a lot of encouragement to start getting involved even if I'm still just learning my way around. I have to show up if I plan to be plugged into the local community and learn what's out there. I'm nervous to pair at this point because I still clutch to templates as I'm building, but you have to let go of the pool wall at some point to swim!

I was pretty impressed with the quality of this conference and the diversity it brought. It had a much more tight-knit feel than other conferences I've attended and it was an easy-going introduction to the larger Ruby community.

This event has been hosted the last 5 years, and this year, unfortunately, was the last. Luckily, the talks were streamed and can be accessed via Youtube. Here are just a few talks I'd recommend checking out.

  • The Most Energy Efficient Supercomputer on the Planet (1:17:00)
  • Human Computer Interaction: The Automation Revolution (3:19:55)
  • He Doesn't Work Here Anymore (4:51:37)
  • MRuby: A Packaging Story Filled with Freedom (6:29:12)
  • Stop the Fanaticism: Choosing the Right Tools for the Job (7:09:31)

 

Brief Firehose Update: I completed my first coding challenges last week and continue to work on improving my sense of OOP. Between that and racking my brain on the next coding challenge, I continue to work on my Nomster Yelp clone project. I'm not as far along as I'd hope due to life events (being away on the conference, getting ready for a new job I'll start in a month, allergies running havoc the last week) but I think I'm making decent progress and I felt pretty good about how much I was able to follow the technical talks at the conference.
