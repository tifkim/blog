I've been studying a lot from home lately, more so since I got my wisdom teeth removed last week. So when I was getting ready to actually head outside the other day, I wasn't used to how long it was taking me to pick out an outfit. #firstworldproblems

Reducing my closet has been something that's long been on my to do list ever since I came across articles like this one from Forbes describing how successful people like Steve Jobs, Mark Zuckerberg, and even President Obama purposely wear the same thing every day.

Image Taken from EliteDaily

There are obvious benefits--saving time in the mornings, avoiding stress and decision fatigue (8 Reasons Successful People are Choosing to Wear the Same Thing Every Day). But another benefit that I hadn't thought of before is consistently looking put-together. Doug Funnie always did look great in his green sweater vest!

Shopping for clothes can also get out of hand. It's reported that women wear an item of clothing on average 7 times before discarding it to avoid being seen in the same outfits (DailyMail), but how many of us have items of clothing we've hardly even worn after buying? I'll raise my hand to this one.

This morning, I took the first step by grabbing more than half the clothes I own and sorting them for Ebay. I'll have to get back to these when I return from traveling, but I'm hoping that once everything's done, I'll have a little pocket change and a lot of relief knowing I'll have less to pack next time we move!
